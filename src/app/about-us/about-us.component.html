FAQs

  • When will I receive my updated policy documents and ID cards if I make changes to my auto policy?
    Your new ID cards will be available on the Confirmation screen immediately after you make a change to your auto policy.
     
    If you're enrolled in eDelivery, you will receive an email within 2-3 days letting you know that your updated policy documents are available online.
     
    If you're not enrolled in eDelivery, you should receive your updated policy documents by U.S. mail in 7-10 days. You can enroll in eDelivery now if you'd like to begin receiving emails when new documents, such as bill statements and policy documents, are available to view online.
     
  • How can I retrieve a saved auto policy change request?
    If you've saved a change request, or if we've saved one for you, you can retrieve it on your Auto Policy page.

    There are a few reasons why a recently saved change request may not be available to view:
    • Saved change requests are only saved for 14 days.
    • Only your 5 most recent change requests are saved.
    • If you save a change request and then make a change to your policy, you will need to start a new change request to ensure the information is accurate.
    • If one of our customer service representatives needed to make changes to the request after you saved it.
  • What information do I need to add a vehicle to my policy?
    When you're adding a vehicle to your policy, it helps to have the following information:
    • Vehicle year, make and model (required)
    • Purchase date
    • Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)
    • Finance (or leasing) company information (if applicable)
     
  • What if I have more than 4 vehicles to insure
    If you'll be insuring more than 4 vehicles, please call for assistance.
  • How can I update my finance or leasing company information?
    You can update this information using your Auto Policy page.

    After you provide us with the updated finance or leasing company information, you can finalize your changes or save the transaction for later.
  • How can I update my Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)?
    Because it may require additional changes to your policy, please call for assistance if you need to update your Vehicle Identification Number (VIN).
  • Should I cancel my policy if I sold my vehicle or totaled it?
    If you sold or totaled your previous vehicle but plan on replacing it within the next 6-8 weeks, you should keep your policy active during that time because:
     
    • We typically will cover any vehicle you borrow or rent in the meantime
    • You will be able to keep the coverage, discounts and any other benefits you remain eligible for when you add your new vehicle
    • You will avoid any down payments that may be necessary to start a new policy
     
  • Should I cancel my policy if my vehicle is inoperable or not running?
    If you expect to have your vehicle fixed or plan on replacing it within the next 6-8 weeks, you should keep your policy active during that time because:
    • We typically will cover any vehicle you borrow or rent in the meantime
    • You will be able to keep the coverage, discounts and any other benefits you remain eligible for when you add your new vehicle
    • You will avoid any down payments that may be necessary to start a new policy
     
    Another factor to consider is that most states require you to have insurance on any vehicle that is registered. If you don't insure your vehicle, you may need to turn in your license plates to avoid fines and/or suspension of your driver's license.
     
  • If my vehicle is in storage or if I'm considering putting it into storage, do I still need to insure it?
    If you plan on putting your vehicle into storage for at least 30 days, we may be able to offer you a storage plan* that will reduce your insurance rate, while still protecting your vehicle. The storage plan will suspend liability coverage, since you won't be driving the vehicle, but will still protect it from damage caused by weather or another storage-related event.
     
    Another factor to consider is that most states require you to have insurance on any vehicle that is registered. If you don't insure your vehicle, you may need to turn in your license plates to avoid fines and/or suspension of your driver's license.
     
    Please call for assistance.
     
    *Storage plans are not available in every state.
